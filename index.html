The Scrum

A rugby league scrum

Why is Scrum needed?

Scrum is designed for the rapid development and delivery of complex, fundamentally new products that are not on the market.

Scrum basics

1. Work is carried out in iterations, which in Scrum are called Sprints

Sprint consists of:

1. Sprint Planning
2. Daily Scrum
3. Sprint Review
4. Sprint Retrospective

2. The result of the work can be considered only what is ready for use

The result of the Sprint is called an Increment, a concrete stepping stone toward the Product Goal.
Work cannot be considered part of an Increment unless it meets the Definition of Done.
The Definition of Done is a formal description of the state of the Increment when it meets the quality measures required for the product.
The moment a Product Backlog item meets the Definition of Done, an Increment is born.

3. The product is developed by a self-managed team

Self-management in Scrum implies independence not only in the choice of who and how will do the work (self-organization), but also in the choice of what exactly to work on in order to achieve the goal of the product.

Scrum Team

The Scrum Team consists of one Scrum Master, one Product Owner, and Developers.

Typically 10 or fewer people.

Developers

The features of the Scrum team that are specifically related to the Developers:
1. "Cross-functionality"
2. Dedicated 100% to work on the product
3. The composition of the team does not change
4. Scrum does not recognize role differences between developers
5. Collaborative work

Product Owner

The Product Owner is responsible for creating a really cool and valuable product for customers.

Scrum Master

The Scrum Master is not a boss, he does not limit or punish. His role is to help the team figure out what obstacles prevent it from working at maximum efficiency. And teach it to remove these obstacles, whether it be process dysfunctions within the team, poor team communication with specific external people / departments, or any outdated regulations that apply throughout the organization.

Scrum Events

Sprint

Sprints are the heartbeat of Scrum, where ideas are turned into value.

During the Sprint:

1. No changes are made that would endanger the Sprint Goal;
2. Quality does not decrease;
3. The Product Backlog is refined as needed;
4. Scope may be clarified and renegotiated with the Product Owner as more is learned.

Sprint Planning

Sprint Planning addresses the following topics:
Topic One: Why is this Sprint valuable?
Topic Two: What can be Done this Sprint?
Topic Three: How will the chosen work get done?

Daily scrum

15-minute meeting at the same time and place every working day of the Sprint.

Sprint Review

The purpose of the Sprint Review is to inspect the outcome of the Sprint and determine future adaptations.
The Sprint Review is the second to last event of the Sprint and is timeboxed to a maximum of four hours for a one-month Sprint.

Sprint Retrospective

The purpose of the Sprint Retrospective is to plan ways to increase quality and effectiveness.
The Sprint Retrospective concludes the Sprint. It is timeboxed to a maximum of three hours for a one-month Sprint.
